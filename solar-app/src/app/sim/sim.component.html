Instructions

Use the controls to adjust the load from 0% to 100% and see how the stored power in the system varies. You can also either supply more power from the wind generator ,if available, or if there is plenty of solar eneergy convert the generator into a fan motor.

If both motor and generator are on then the direction of current will depend on whether the energy being produced from the wind is greater than the energy already in the system.

The stored energy should not exceed 2.5v but needs to be above about 1.7v in order for the load (red LED) to switch on, even dimly.

The orange LED prevents serious overcharging of the storage device and represents energy wasted by too much supply with a low demand.

Controls

Solar Volts

The solar volts gives an indication of the power being provided through solar photovoltaic energy

Store Volts

The store volts indicates the available power to drive the load or the motor. As solar or wind energy is fed into the system the store voltage should rise. If neither energy source is providing power then the load must be powered from stored energy.

Load Current

The load current indicates the power being used by a hypothetical consumer represented by the red LED. In reality this could be a home, a factory, or any other utility connected to the grid.

Generator / Motor

The generator high voltage and generator low voltage shows the potential difference across the generator. Depending on how the motor and generator switchs are set this could be providing wind energy as a generator or consuming energy as a motor. In renewable energy systems it is useful to be able to control the direction of power depending on supply and demand fluctuations
